Wireframes And Their Purpose

The wireframes played an integral role in how the project has been structured. Furthermore, they also helped in the CSS styling as well as the general syntax of the HTML.

Wireframes And Specific Pages

As mentioned above, wireframes helped me with the structure of the pages of the site and -- as such -- I was able to determine what kind of layout I wanted quite quickly. This helped me set out a plan in terms of how specific pages should be displayed.

In this vein, the wireframes in the carousel below showcase:

  • The 'AdoptCat' app page.
  • The main articles page.
  • The index page
  • An example of how article pages would be structured
  • The 'How to Feed Your Cat' page.

Wireframes And Their Overall Impact on The Project

I knew pretty early on how I wanted the pages to be structured, so the design of the wireframes was mostly hassle-free, though I struggled with tools such as Figma. Therefore, I chose to design them in Paint, which I found to be the most convenient method.

Specifically, Paint allowed me to focus on showcasing what I wanted the pages to look like in a visual way without having to worry about setting anything up or learning new tools. This helped me to start working on the final project from an early date onwards. This ensured that I had the bare bones of the project ready with ample time to test, gather feedback from friends / family and make any necessary adjustments.

Obviously, the look and feel of the pages has changed slightly, but having a general outline helped me to organise my workload and devote time to the more challenging aspects of the projects - such as the Javascript -- more efficiently.
